Session 1 of 4
Brand Foundation
We establish who Stronghold is at its core — the mission, the model, what makes it different, and the emotional territory the brand owns.

📝 All fields are required. Complete this session before moving forward.

Duration: 90 minutes · Remote via Zoom or Google Meet
Pre-Work — Complete Before the Session
Individual Prep
Answer these independently before joining. Sessions without pre-work completed will run long and produce weaker outputs.
Warm-Up · 5 min
2 Words
Describe Stronghold in exactly 2 words — no explanation, no hedging. First instinct only. Go around the room, read them out loud, and note recurring words. Recurring words become the brand.
Exercise 1.1 · 15 min
The One Sentence
If you had 10 seconds to explain Stronghold to a stranger, what would you say? Each person writes independently, then share and compare. Find the recurring elements and build a combined version.
Exercise 1.2 · 15 min
What We Are NOT
Define the brand by exclusion — what Stronghold refuses to be. List 3 organizations Stronghold is different from, explain how, then agree on 5 firm "We Are NOT" statements as a group.
Exercise 1.3 · 20 min
The Origin Story
Surface the founding moment and core conviction that makes Stronghold real. Tell the story of how Stronghold started — the specific moment the decision was made. Write the 3-sentence version together.
Exercise 1.4 · 20 min
Brand Pillars Validation
Validate the four brand pillars from the Brand OS against the team's own experience. Vote for each: Confirmed / Partially / No — and note what's missing or what needs adjusting.
Exercise 1.5 · 20 min
The Pivot Story
Write the narrative that explains Stronghold's evolution from tactical rescue to medical humanitarian work — as a natural continuation, not a hard pivot. Identify the constant, the change, and build the 3-act statement Ephraim can tell anywhere.
Exercise 1.6 · 15 min
Our Motto
Agree on the single line that captures Stronghold's essence — the phrase Ephraim can say on any podcast, any stage, or any email and have it land perfectly. Three options from the Brand OS: "A Few Good Men" / "Charity With Dignity" / "We Run Toward the Fire."
Session 2 of 4
Visual Identity
We define how Stronghold looks — colors, typography, photography direction, and the visual language that makes the brand instantly recognizable.

📝 All fields are required. Complete this session before moving forward.

Duration: 90 minutes · Remote via Zoom or Google Meet
Pre-Work — Complete Before the Session
Visual Prep
Collect images, note brands you respect, and review the Brand OS color/typography before joining.
Exercise 2.1 · 20 min
Image Sort
Screen-share the collected image folder. Each person picks their top 3 and explains why in one sentence. Group images by theme: Documentary / Operator / Community / Landscape. Note where there's the most agreement — that's your primary visual language.
Exercise 2.2 · 20 min
Color Palette Review
Walk through each color from the Brand OS: name, hex, role, and usage rule. Team votes: Confirmed / Adjust / Replace. Document any changes with reasoning.
Exercise 2.3 · 15 min
Typography Gut Check
Review sample text using Inter Bold, Bebas Neue, and Libre Baskerville Italic. Key debate: Does Bebas Neue feel too military? Not enough? Confirm or identify an alternative.
Exercise 2.4 · 15 min
Photography Rules
Set firm rules for what Stronghold imagery does and does not look like. Review the DO / DON'T table from the Brand OS, then add rules specific to Stronghold's real operational situation.

Session 4 of 4
Content Strategy
We define what Stronghold talks about, how often, and how to structure messages for different audiences and channels. This feeds directly into the Content Pillars Framework and Messaging Matrix.

📝 All fields are required. Complete this session before moving forward.

Duration: 90 minutes · Remote via Zoom or Google Meet
Pre-Work — Complete Before the Session
Content Audit Prep
Review your last 30 days of Instagram, list the questions your supporters ask most, and inventory your story types before joining.
Warm-Up · 10 min
The Villain
Every strong brand has a villain — not a competitor, but the thing they exist to fight against. Aid dependency? Bureaucracy that moves too slow while people die? Donor guilt culture? The idea that charity should feel like sacrifice? Each person names one. The villain that gets the most energy in the room IS the villain.
Exercise 4.1 · 20 min
Content Reality Check
Map what Stronghold actually produces vs. what the brand strategy calls for. Review the last 30 days of Instagram, calculate the actual % mix vs. the target, and identify the gaps.
Exercise 4.2 · 25 min
Audience Messaging Matrix
Define the key message for each audience type. For each segment: what is the one thing they need to believe to take action? Write the core message in 1–2 sentences.
Exercise 4.3 · 20 min
Story Bank Categories
Build the story capture system so field content doesn't get lost. For each category: what's the minimum viable capture? Assign a team member and agree on where stories are stored before they reach Indigo Tones.
Exercise 4.4 · 15 min
Channel Cadence Agreement
Lock in realistic content cadence the team can actually sustain. Recommended from the Brand OS: Instagram 4–5x/week, Email 1–2x/month, YouTube 1–2x/week. Reality-check against what's achievable right now — it can increase over time.
Exercise 4.5 · 20 min
Talking Points for the Field
Build the ready-to-use proof points, analogies, and phrases Ephraim and the team can deploy in any public-facing context — podcast, donor call, media interview, or stage. Use real numbers. Write the 90-second pitch word for word.
